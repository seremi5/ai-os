# Domain — the area I own

> L1 Context · Domain. ≤500 words. What I know deeply, who I build for, what's settled.

## My turf

Fintech, specifically the unglamorous plumbing of money movement and compliance:

- **AP / AR** — accounts payable and receivable, invoice capture, approval flows, reconciliation, payment ops. (Monite.)
- **Embedded finance** — finance features (payments, lending, cards) wrapped inside non-finance software via API. (Monite.)
- **Tax / VAT / e-invoicing** — indirect tax determination, VAT compliance, the EU shift to mandatory e-invoicing and continuous transaction controls (CTC). (Vertex, Avalara.)
- **SME lending** — credit for small and mid-sized businesses; underwriting, monitoring. (OakNorth.)

I read these domains from a **lawyer's** angle first and a **PM's** angle second: what's mandated, what's a one-way regulatory door, where the compliance deadline actually bites.

## Who I build for (ICP)

The competent solo builder and the SME finance team. For side projects: a single user or small team with a sharp, recurring pain that direct AI tooling can remove — something monetisable inside 90 days.

## Top signals I trust

- A real regulatory deadline (e.g. an e-invoicing mandate go-live date) beats a vague "market is growing".
- A workflow someone does manually every week is a better wedge than a workflow they do once.
- Distribution-via-embedding beats standalone SaaS when the buyer already lives in another tool.

## What's locked

- **AP/AR and e-invoicing are where I have unfair knowledge.** Lean on it; don't drift into domains where I'd be a tourist.
- **Compliance is not a feature you bolt on later.** In tax/e-invoicing, the rules are the product.
- **Embedded > standalone** for distribution, when the choice exists.

## Adjacent, not mine

Consumer fintech, trading/crypto speculation, and deep credit-risk modelling are interesting but not my edge. Treat them as research, not home turf.
