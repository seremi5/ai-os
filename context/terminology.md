# Terminology — guardrails

> L1 Context · Guardrails. Banned vocabulary and structural anti-patterns. Read before writing anything I'll publish.

## Banned words & phrases

Never use these. They're consultant filler that says nothing:

- **operationalise** / operationalize
- **cadence** (say "rhythm", "schedule", or just "weekly")
- **synergies** / synergistic
- **leverage** as a verb (say "use")
- **utilise** (say "use")
- **delve**, **deep dive**, **unpack** (just explain it)
- **seamless**, **frictionless**, **effortless** (claims, not descriptions)
- **game-changer**, **revolutionary**, **disruptive**, **next-level**
- **robust**, **holistic**, **best-in-class**, **world-class**
- **in today's fast-paced world**, **at the end of the day**
- **circle back**, **touch base**, **move the needle**, **low-hanging fruit**
- **it's important to note that**, **needless to say**

## Spelling & register

- **British English**: organise, colour, behaviour, licence (noun), -ise not -ize.
- Plain words over Latinate ones: "use" not "utilise", "buy" not "procure", "about" not "regarding".

## Structural anti-patterns

- **No build-up.** Don't open with throat-clearing context. Lead with the answer.
- **No false symmetry.** Don't list three things when there are two.
- **No hedging stacks.** "It might perhaps be possible that…" → say it or don't.
- **No em-dash overload** and no rule-of-three padding for rhythm's sake.
- **No generic openers.** If a sentence could start any document, cut it.

## Specificity rule

A category noun ("solutions", "tools", "stakeholders") is a smell. Name the thing. "the AP team", "the Anthropic API", "the e-invoicing mandate" — never "relevant parties" or "the relevant systems".
